# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary visitor is a VC investor evaluating NanoCapital as a credible technology company and potential portfolio-grade partner. They arrive with high skepticism because the surface includes fintech, USDT, Web3, AI, and commerce claims, so the page must help them quickly distinguish experienced operators from speculative or unserious projects.

## Product Purpose

NanoCapital presents payment, commerce, AI, blockchain, and custom software delivery capability in one landing-page narrative. The site exists to make an investor understand what NanoCapital can build, see evidence of prior execution, and feel confident enough to start a direct conversation.

## Positioning

NanoCapital is an experienced technical team that can turn payment, commerce, and Web3 infrastructure into real deployable business systems.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary and secondary CTA: primary is to request a proposal or solution demo; secondary is to view the portfolio and schedule a consultation.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: NanoCapital builds credible payment, commerce, and Web3 systems with proven delivery experience.
- Belief ladder: NanoCapital is real and operational; the team has shipped serious technical systems before; the product areas fit investor-grade fintech and commerce opportunities; the commercial terms and compliance posture are transparent; a conversation is worth taking.
- Proof on hand: the current page lists delivered or portfolio projects across VAISEN Mainnet, ILOVEKOREA.AI, BUYZONE Protocol, AutoBlog, SimplePayX ERP, Oracle Predict, RealSun, SMFI, Mission Hunter, and HUB Membership.

## Brand Personality

Reliable, experienced, and skilled. The voice should feel serious, specific, and investment-aware, with enough technical confidence to earn trust without sounding inflated.

## Anti-references

The site must not feel like a scam, a beginner portfolio, a speculative crypto pitch, a generic Web3 template, or a vague agency page that makes broad claims without proof. Avoid exaggerated return language, hype-heavy token language, fake urgency, anonymous-looking visuals, and anything that weakens trust.

## Design Principles

- Lead with credibility before ambition: every major claim should be backed by evidence, specificity, or clear commercial structure.
- Make the investor's skepticism useful: anticipate concerns around USDT, Web3, compliance, and delivery capability instead of hiding them.
- Show experienced execution: portfolio, process, timelines, and terms should read as operational artifacts, not decorative marketing copy.
- Keep the page bilingual-ready with Korean priority: layout, navigation, and copy patterns must support Korean as a first-class language.
- Prefer precise confidence over loud hype: the brand can look ambitious, but the language and structure must remain trustworthy.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Korean language priority is required. The site should support Korean copy without layout breakage, preserve readable line lengths, and avoid text treatments that make Korean typography feel secondary. Motion should respect reduced-motion preferences, and trust-critical content should remain readable with strong contrast.
